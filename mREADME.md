## Question 1
Data: is any raw information that represents a fact, measurement, 
or observation about something or someone.
Exemples: A number → 42, 3.99, 1000
          An image, a sound, a video

## Question 2
Structured Data: It is well-organized data, arranged in a specific format, such as a table with rows and columns. 
It is easy to read, search, and analyze.

exemple1: 
Every transaction has a date, an amount, and a description. It always follows the same format, 
row after row.
example2: A customer database
When you sign up on a website, your information is saved in a very specific order: your first name, 
your last name, your email address, your date of birth, and your country. Every single user follows 
that exact same pattern. Nothing is out of place, nothing is missing. It's predictable and consistent.

Unstructured Data: This is data with no fixed format, hard to organize in a table. It's more complex 
to analyze but represents the vast majority of data in the real world.

Example 1 — An email
An email contains free text, attachments, and images. You can't easily fit it into a table.
Example 2 — A YouTube video
A video contains images, sound, and comments. There's no fixed structure — it's just a block of raw 
information.

## Question 3

A schema is simply the blueprint or plan that defines how your data is organized.
It describes:

What information will be stored
What type that information is (text, number, date…)
What rules it must follow
example:
First Name → text
Last Name → text
Date of Birth → date
Phone Number → numbers only
Reason for visit → text

## Explain the difference between
📋 A List
A list is a collection of items stored in a specific order. You access each item by its position (0, 1, 2...).

📖 A Dictionary
A dictionary is a collection of items stored with a label. You access each item by its key (a name you give it).
